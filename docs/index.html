Japanese population in Japan from 2013 to 2017

Source: Jūminhyō (registry of current residential addresses)

  • It’s official: Japan’s population is dramatically shrinking (The Washington Post, 2016)

Expat population in Japan from 2013 to 2017

Source: Jūminhyō

  • 2.3M expats is about 1.8% of total population.
  • In UN report, which estimates foreign-born population, Japan's 1.9% of total population is ranked 66th among 229 countries. And 2.4M is ranked 25th.

Population by prefecture as of January 2017

Source: Jūminhyō, Orange: Japanese, Blue: expat.

  • The highest ratio of expats is 3.6% in Tokyo.

Population change between 2016 and 2017

Source: Jūminhyō, Orange: Japanese, Blue: expat.

  • Tokyo, for instance, 836k Japanese people moved in, 112k were born, 763k moved out, 113k dead, there are some miscellaneous numbers, overall about 77k more people live in Tokyo compared to 2016.
  • Saitama, Chiba and Kanagawa are part of the Greater Tokyo Area.
  • Aichi is where Toyota Motor Corporation headquartered in.
  • Okinawa is the only prefecture where more people were born (17k) than dead (12k).
